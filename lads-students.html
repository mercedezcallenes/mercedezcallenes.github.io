POLS 230 Exploring Latin American Politics (Fall 2025), LADS Minor – Global Affairs Department at Trinity Washington University

Students

Students in the Latin American Development Studies minor engage Latin America through an interdisciplinary lens that brings together politics, development, gender, migration, environment, culture, and social justice. The program is designed to help students connect classroom learning to the major challenges facing the region and to broader global questions about inequality, democracy, and human dignity.

LADS students strengthen their analytical, writing, and research skills while developing a deeper understanding of Latin America’s historical trajectories and contemporary realities. Through coursework, faculty mentoring, and discussion-based learning, students are encouraged to think critically, communicate clearly, and apply their knowledge to real-world issues.

Many students in the minor pursue projects that reflect Trinity’s mission of ethical leadership and engaged learning. Their interests often include migration, gender and development, environmental justice, indigenous rights, democratic participation, and the social consequences of economic change. The minor complements a wide range of majors and supports students as they prepare for graduate study, public service, nonprofit work, advocacy, and internationally oriented careers.

Student Engagement

LADS students participate in a wide variety of intellectually and professionally enriching experiences. These may include:

  • Faculty-mentored research on Latin America and global development
  • Class presentations and independent analytical projects
  • Conference participation and public-facing academic work
  • Interdisciplinary conversations linking policy, history, economics, and culture
  • Critical reflection on social justice, sustainability, and civic responsibility

Student work in the minor reflects curiosity, purpose, and a willingness to examine how local realities in Latin America connect to wider regional and global processes.

Graduates

LADS graduates carry this interdisciplinary preparation into graduate school, public service, nonprofit work, research, and community-based leadership. Their experiences in the minor help them develop a broader international perspective while remaining grounded in the human dimensions of policy and development.
