Latin American Development Studies (LADS) is an interdisciplinary minor dedicated to examining the political, economic, and social dynamics shaping Latin America and its diasporas.

The program introduces students to critical perspectives on development, inequality, and power, with particular attention to issues such as gender, migration, environmental justice, indigenous rights, and social movements.

LADS encourages students to think comparatively and historically about development processes while engaging with contemporary challenges facing the region. Courses draw from multiple disciplines, including political science, sociology, history, and anthropology.

As director of the program, I work to create an intellectually rigorous and socially engaged curriculum that connects academic study with real-world issues and prepares students for global citizenship.
